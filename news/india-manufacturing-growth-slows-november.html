India's Manufacturing Growth Slows in November

Published Nov 25, 2025

PMI Dips but Stays in Expansion

The seasonally adjusted purchasing managers' index slipped to 53.2 from October's 55.1, still signaling expansion but at the slowest pace in six months. Survey respondents cited weaker export flows and a focus on running down finished goods stocks after the festive season.

Textiles, engineering goods, and chemicals reported fewer new orders, while food processing and electronics retained healthy pipelines thanks to domestic consumption.

Input Costs and Hiring

Input price pressures eased, especially for metals and transportation, prompting some manufacturers to pass on savings via promotional discounts. Hiring remained broadly stable, with large conglomerates substituting contract labor with full-time roles in logistics and maintenance.

Industry associations urged the government to extend export credit support and expedite port infrastructure upgrades so that shipments can rebound in early 2026.
