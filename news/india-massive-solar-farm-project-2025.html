The central government has approved construction of a large-scale solar farm covering over 5,000 hectares aimed at boosting rural electrification and meeting renewable energy goals. The project is expected to generate enough power to illuminate more than 2 million homes in under-served regions within the next 2 years.

Officials said the initiative aligns with India's net-zero ambitions and will also reduce dependence on fossil fuels. Local communities will benefit from employment opportunities and improved infrastructure.

Environmental experts welcomed the move, citing its potential to reduce carbon emissions significantly. They recommended accompanying ecosystem-impact assessments and sustainable land-use planning to safeguard biodiversity and water resources.
