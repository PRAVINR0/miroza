Quantum Computing Breakthrough: 1 Million Qubits Reached

By Tech Desk | Updated: Dec 06, 2025, 11:00 IST

Scientists at the Global Quantum Institute have announced a historic milestone: the successful operation of a quantum processor with over 1 million stable qubits. This breakthrough brings us significantly closer to fault-tolerant quantum computing.

"This is the 'Wright Brothers' moment for quantum computing," said Dr. Elena Rostova, lead researcher. "We have moved from experimental toys to machines that can solve problems impossible for classical supercomputers."

The implications are vast, ranging from drug discovery and materials science to breaking current encryption standards. Governments worldwide are already ramping up their quantum security protocols in response.
