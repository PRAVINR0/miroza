India Economy

India's GDP Expected to Grow 7.3% in Q2

Published Nov 25, 2025

Advertisement

Forecast Driven by Urban Demand

Senior officials told— MIROZA that advance estimates point to a 7.3% gross domestic product expansion for the July–September quarter, with household consumption remaining steady in metropolitan regions. Sectors such as automobiles, electronics, and premium services are reporting double-digit volume growth that could offset weakness in rural demand.

The finance ministry said direct tax collections were up 18% year-on-year for the quarter, signaling higher profitability across listed firms. Economists added that public capital expenditure on logistics, rail, and defense manufacturing continued to anchor investment momentum.

Risks from Global Slowdown

Despite the upbeat projection, authorities cautioned that volatile crude prices and shrinking export orders could weigh on Q3 performance. The commerce department said it is monitoring orders from the US and Europe, where retailers have trimmed purchase plans for winter.

Analysts expect the Reserve Bank of India to maintain its pause on policy rates in December while it evaluates inflation trends through the festival season. Rating agencies, meanwhile, maintain India will remain one of the fastest-growing major economies in FY26.
