New AI Regulations for 2025

By Legal Desk | Updated: Dec 06, 2025, 08:45 IST

In a landmark decision, the United Nations has ratified the Global AI Safety Accord, a comprehensive set of regulations designed to govern the development and deployment of advanced artificial intelligence systems. The accord, signed by 190 nations, comes into effect on January 1, 2026.

The new rules focus on transparency, accountability, and safety. Developers of "frontier models" will be required to undergo rigorous safety testing by independent auditors before releasing their products to the public. Additionally, all AI-generated content must be clearly watermarked to prevent misinformation.

"This is a necessary step to ensure that AI benefits all of humanity," said UN Secretary-General. "We cannot allow technology to outpace our ability to govern it."

Impact on Tech Giants

Tech giants have reacted with mixed feelings. While some welcome the clarity, others argue that the regulations could stifle innovation. However, most agree that a unified global framework is preferable to a patchwork of national laws.
