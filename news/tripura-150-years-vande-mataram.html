Tripura Celebrates 150 Years of "Vande Mataram"

Published Nov 25, 2025

Statewide Cultural Programs

The Tripura government flagged off a week-long celebration featuring classical dance, poetry readings, and an archival exhibition on Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay's life. Performances were staged simultaneously in Agartala, Udaipur, and Kailashahar to ensure maximum citizen participation.

Schoolchildren formed 150-member choirs that rendered "Vande Mataram" in Bengali, Kokborok, and Hindi, underscoring the state's linguistic diversity.

Heritage Walks and Panels

Heritage walks retraced the nationalist movement's footprint in Tripura, while historians discussed the song's role in India's freedom struggle. Artists created murals celebrating women's contributions to the independence movement.

The Chief Minister announced scholarships for young musicians preserving patriotic repertoire and said the celebration will culminate with a drone show over the Ujjayanta Palace grounds.
