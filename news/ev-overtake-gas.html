Electric Vehicles Overtake Gas Cars

By Mike Ross | Updated: Dec 06, 2025, 10:15 IST

In a historic milestone for the automotive industry, global sales of electric vehicles (EVs) have officially surpassed those of internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles for the first time in history. Data released by the Global Auto Alliance confirms that EVs accounted for 52% of all new car sales in Q3 2025.

"This is the tipping point we have been waiting for," said industry analyst Jane Doe. "The combination of government incentives, improved battery technology, and a wider range of affordable models has finally pushed EVs into the mainstream."

Major automakers who pivoted early to electric platforms are seeing record profits, while those who lagged behind are scrambling to catch up. The shift is particularly pronounced in Europe and China, where strict emissions regulations have accelerated the transition.

Infrastructure Challenges Remain

Despite the sales boom, challenges remain. Charging infrastructure in rural areas still lags behind urban centers, and grid capacity is a growing concern. However, with massive infrastructure bills passing in major economies, experts are optimistic that these hurdles will be overcome in the next few years.
