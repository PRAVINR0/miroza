Practicing Mindfulness in the Digital Age

By Sarah Jenkins | Updated: Dec 06, 2025

LONDON â€” In a world of constant notifications, endless scrolling, and digital noise, finding peace of mind can feel impossible. Yet, mindfulness is more important now than ever.

"We are constantly in a state of 'continuous partial attention'," says psychologist Dr. James Miller. "This fragments our focus and increases anxiety."

The solution isn't necessarily to abandon technology, but to use it intentionally. Digital detoxes, app limits, and mindful notification settings can help reclaim our attention.

Simple practices like conscious breathing before checking email, or taking a "tech-free" walk in nature, can reset our nervous system and restore clarity.
