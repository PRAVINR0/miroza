Mars Mission: The First 100 Days

By Sarah Jenkins | Updated: Dec 06, 2025, 14:00 IST

The first 100 days of the Mars colony have been a testament to human resilience and ingenuity. From the initial landing to the establishment of the first permanent habitat, the journey has been fraught with challenges and triumphs.

Commander Lewis and her team have successfully deployed the solar arrays, ensuring a steady power supply for the base. "The dust storms were a concern," Lewis reported in her daily log, "but the new shielding technology has held up beyond our expectations."

Water extraction from the subsurface ice has also begun, providing not only drinking water but also the raw materials for fuel production. This marks a critical step towards self-sustainability.

Life on the Red Planet

Daily life for the colonists is a rigorous schedule of maintenance, scientific experiments, and exercise to counteract the lower gravity. Despite the hardships, morale remains high. The greenhouse module has produced its first harvest of radishes and lettuce, a small but symbolic victory.

As we look towards the next phase of the mission, the focus will shift to expanding the habitat and preparing for the arrival of the second wave of colonists in 2027.
